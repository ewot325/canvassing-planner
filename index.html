      How this works

      Finding the best districts to canvass

      Two “🎯 targeting” shadings highlight the election districts that are the best use of canvassing time. Both weigh down places you've already canvassed (the more prior canvassing, the lower the priority — though a very high-value district can still rank well). Pick whichever matches your goal for the week:

      • 🎯 Persuasion targets — districts where lots of people actually vote but the team hasn't canvassed much. Best when the goal is to talk to reliable voters and win them over.
      • 🎯 Turnout (GOTV) targets — districts with many registered Democrats who don't reliably vote, again where you haven't canvassed. Best when the goal is to get supporters who often stay home to actually show up.

      Darker color = higher priority. Colors are spread by rank, so the top and bottom districts are always easy to tell apart. Click any district to see its persuasion and GOTV rank.

      The 🎯 Top targets list on the left ranks the highest-priority districts for whichever goal you pick, using the same calculation. For each district it multiplies how much room there is to help — the number of voters (persuasion) or registered Democrats who don't reliably vote (turnout) — by how little it's been canvassed so far. Districts with lots of opportunity and little prior canvassing rise to the top; each row shows its registered Democrats and the person-days already spent there.

      A note on demographics & polls: we tested predicting each district's support from Census demographics and the campaign polls, but NY-12 is too uniform for that to work reliably — so the targeting uses solid fundamentals (registered Democrats, turnout, and coverage) instead. The polls still guide overall strategy (e.g. lots of undecided voters, a gender gap worth messaging on).

      Using the site

      Left panel

      • Search — type any address or place to find it on the map (you can add it to a shift too).
      • Color the districts by — switch the shading (persuasion targets, turnout/GOTV targets, 2026 primary results, 2025 mayor, turnout, or coverage).
      • Show on the map — toggle the district lines & labels (election + assembly districts), neighborhoods, subway stops, priority poll sites, early voting sites, and supermarkets.
      • Weather & Events — NYC weather and this week's in-district community events (farmers markets, fairs). Click an event to find it and add it to a shift.

      The map

      • Click an election district to see its registered Democrats, its persuasion & GOTV targeting rank, the 2026 primary result, and 2025 mayoral result.
      • Click a supermarket, subway stop, or search result for its info too.

      Right panel — plan the week

      • Use ‹ › to choose the week. Each day has an AM and a PM shift — click one to select it.
      • Then click anything on the map and choose Add to shift. Click it again (or the ✕ on its chip) to remove it.
      • Print / Save PDF or Copy as text to share the plan.
