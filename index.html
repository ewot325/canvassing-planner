    How this works

    How “Priority for next week” is calculated

    The default shading highlights the election districts that are the best use of canvassing time: places with many Democratic voters that the team has not canvassed much yet.

    For each district it combines two things:

    • Turnout — how many people voted in the 2025 Democratic primary (more voters = more valuable).
    • Coverage — how much canvassing that area has already received.

    Districts that are high turnout but low coverage get the darkest red. Already-covered or low-turnout districts fade to light. Colors are spread by rank, so the highest and lowest are always easy to tell apart.

    Using the site

    Left panel

    • Search — type any address or place to find it on the map (you can add it to a shift too).
    • Color the districts by — switch the shading (priority, 2026 primary results, 2025 mayor, turnout, or coverage).
    • Show on the map — toggle neighborhoods, subway stops, priority poll sites, early voting sites, and supermarkets.

    The map

    • Click an election district to see its registered Democrats, 2026 primary result, and 2025 mayoral result.
    • Click a supermarket, subway stop, or search result for its info too.

    Right panel — plan the week

    • Use ‹ › to choose the week. Each day has an AM and a PM shift — click one to select it.
    • Then click anything on the map and choose Add to shift. Click it again (or the ✕ on its chip) to remove it.
    • Print / Save PDF or Copy as text to share the plan.
